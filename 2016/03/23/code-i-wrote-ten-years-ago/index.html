What will i say to the code I wrote ten years ago?

2016-03-23
general, thoughts

I don’t know where you are. In all probability you are either lying in a hibernated state in some repository or just being thrown into oblivion.

Maybe you are still being maintained. You just might have been completely transformed. Well, in that case, at least you are living your dream.

I look back at the days when I worked with you. I was your coach, your mentor and you were my prodigy. I could have done so much for you. I could have structured you differently. I could have made you more modular. Bolstering you with unit tests could have given you so much more stability. I could have made you more readable so that the next person maintaining you wouldn’t have torn you apart. I could have made you strong enough to face bugs. I could have made you run in real time environments rather than keeping you in training for so long.

Now, I am not apologetic. I just didn’t know any better. We were both learning, both evolving. I don’t know if we will ever meet again. And even if we do, I don’t know if I will be able to recognize you with all the transformations you must have gone through. I have over the years gotten better too. I have new mentees now. New prodigies just like you. I have been able to train them smarter, stronger and harder. They don’t spend months in training, rather they get onto the real field, fail fast and then get back to improve. I have been able to mentor them better because I am collaborating with other coaches. I don’t work in silos anymore. I don’t get embarrass when one of my mentees runs and fails. I instead get back to training and make them better.

None of this would have been possible without you. With all that I learned while coaching you.

So long, my old friend.
