Point by Point - Scrum Vs Kanban

2016-04-17
kanban, point by point, project management, scrum

Scrum vs Kanban

  1. Scrum and Kanban are both process tools.

  2. Scrum is more prescriptive than Kanban.

  3. Both work on the philosophy of “Don’t limit yourself to one tool!”.

  4. Scrum prescribes roles whereas Kanban does not prescribe any roles at all.

  5. Both believe in “less is more”.

  6. Scrum prescribes timeboxed iterations.

  7. Kanban limits WIP per workflow state, Scrum limits WIP per iteration.

  8. Scrum resists change within an iteration.

  9. Scrum board is reset between each iteration whereas Kanban board is persistent.

  10. Scrum prescribes cross-functional teams.

  11. Scrum backlog items must fit in a sprint.

  12. Scrum prescribes estimation and velocity.

  13. Both allow working on multiple products simultaneously.

  14. Both are Lean and Agile.

  15. Scrum prescribes a prioritized product backlog.

  16. In Scrum, daily meetings are prescribed.

  17. In Scrum, burndown charts are prescribed.
